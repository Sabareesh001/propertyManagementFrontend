# Customer Support Knowledge Base

> This document holds the customer-facing answer content the support chatbot draws from. It is written in plain language for tenants and owners. It deliberately contains **no technical or internal details** (no endpoints, status numbers, field names, or admin mechanics). Pair it with `system-prompt.md`, which defines how the assistant must behave.
>
> Whenever a specific fee percentage, timeline, or limit isn't stated here, the assistant should say it doesn't have that exact detail and offer to connect the customer with human support, rather than inventing a number.

## 0. The Big Picture — How the Whole Platform Fits Together

Before the individual flows, here's how everything connects, so the assistant can orient a confused customer quickly.

### The tenant journey, end to end

1. **Sign up** as a tenant and **verify your email** so you can log in.
2. **Get identity-verified** by our team (upload your documents once) — this unlocks the ability to send rental requests.
3. **Browse and compare** listings, and optionally **schedule a site visit** to see a place in person.
4. **Send a rental request** to the owner with your proposed dates and amounts.
5. The owner **accepts** your request and **draws up a lease.**
6. You **review and sign** the lease agreement; after a final check it becomes **active.**
7. You **pay rent and other charges** as they come due — online by card or by recording a manual payment.
8. If something breaks, you **raise a complaint** on your active lease and track it to resolution.
9. When the term ends, the lease **expires automatically.** If you need to leave early, you **request a cancellation.**

### The owner journey, end to end

1. **Sign up** as an owner (or add the owner ability to a tenant account) and **verify your email.**
2. **Get identity-verified** by our team — this unlocks the ability to list properties.
3. **Create a property listing,** upload photos, attach the required ownership document, and **submit it for verification.**
4. Once our team **verifies** the listing, it goes **live** for tenants to find.
5. **Receive rental requests,** review them, and **accept or reject** them.
6. From an accepted request, **draw up the lease,** attach the agreement, and send it through review and signing until it's **active.**
7. **Apply charges** (like monthly rent) and **track payments.** Optionally set up **online card payouts.**
8. **Handle complaints** on your properties and work them to resolution with the tenant.
9. End a lease early with a **cancellation** when needed, or let it **expire** naturally at the end of its term.

### The core idea

Almost every important step — verification, a listing going live, a lease, and a cancellation — involves a short review by **our team** before it takes effect. This is deliberate: it confirms identities, keeps listings genuine, and makes sure both sides agree in writing before anything binding happens. That's why you'll see things move through stages like "under review" rather than happening instantly.

## 1. About the Platform

### What is this platform?

It's an online property rental platform that connects two kinds of people:

- **Tenants** — people looking to rent a home.
- **Owners** — people who list their homes for rent.

The platform guides both sides through the whole rental journey in one place: creating an account, getting verified, browsing and comparing listings, scheduling visits, sending and reviewing rental requests, signing a lease, paying rent and other charges, raising maintenance issues, and ending a lease when the time comes. Everything is tracked and time-stamped so both parties always know exactly where things stand.

### Who can use it?

Anyone can sign up. When you register you choose whether you're joining as a tenant (to rent) or an owner (to list). You can start as a tenant and later add the ability to list properties too — you don't need a second account. See **"Can I be both a tenant and an owner?"** below.

### What can I do without an account?

You can **browse listings and view property details** without signing in. To do anything that involves you personally — scheduling a visit, sending a rental request, listing a property, signing a lease, or paying — you'll need an account and, for most of those, to be verified.

### Is there a cost to use it?

Creating an account and browsing listings is free. When money changes hands — for example paying rent online by card — a small fee may apply, and the exact amount is always shown clearly before you confirm the payment. If you'd like a specific fee confirmed in advance, our support team can help.

### What does "verified" mean, and why does it keep coming up?

The platform is built around trust between strangers, so it confirms a few things along the way. "Verified" can refer to your **email** (that the address is yours), your **identity** (that our team has approved your documents), a **listing** (that our team has confirmed the property is genuine), or a **lease/cancellation agreement** (that the paperwork has been reviewed). Section 5 explains how to tell these apart.

## 2. Creating an Account (Sign Up)

### How do I sign up?

1. Go to the **Register** page.
2. Enter your email, a password, your first and last name, your phone number, and your date of birth.
3. Choose whether you're joining as a **tenant** (to rent) or an **owner** (to list properties).
4. Submit the form. We'll send a verification link to your email — you'll need to confirm it before you can log in (see **Email Verification**).

### What are the password requirements?

Your password needs to be reasonably strong — a good mix of upper- and lower-case letters, at least one number, and a special character, and long enough to be secure. The sign-up form tells you if your password doesn't meet the requirements so you can adjust it before submitting. Choose something unique that you don't use on other sites.

### Do I have to be a certain age?

Yes — you must be at least 18 years old to create an account. You'll enter your date of birth when you register, and it needs to show you're 18 or older.

### What information do I need to register, and why?

A valid email address, a strong password, your legal first and last name, a contact phone number, and your date of birth. Use accurate details — your name and contact information are used later when you get identity-verified and when leases are drawn up, so mismatches can slow those steps down.

### I already started as one role — did I pick the wrong one?

Don't worry. If you registered as a tenant but want to list a property, you can add the owner ability later without making a new account (see **Roles**). You don't need to delete anything or start over.

### Can I use the same email for two accounts?

No — each account uses its own email address. If you try to register with an email that's already in use, sign in with that email instead, or use "Forgot password" if you can't remember the password.

## 3. Email Verification

### Why do I need to verify my email?

Verifying your email confirms that the address belongs to you. **You need to verify your email before you can log in for the first time.** It's a quick, one-time step that protects your account.

### How do I verify my email?

When you register, we send a message to your email with a verification link. Open the email and click the link — that confirms your address and lets you log in. If you don't see it within a few minutes, check your spam or junk folder and make sure you entered your email correctly at sign-up.

### The verification link expired or didn't work — what do I do?

Verification links are only valid for a limited time after you register. If yours has expired, never arrived, or gives an error, use the **"Resend verification email"** option (it also appears on the login screen if you try to log in before verifying). We'll send a fresh link to your email. Always use the newest link — older ones stop working once a new one is sent.

### I try to log in and it says my email isn't verified.

That just means the email confirmation step hasn't been completed yet. Look for our verification email and click the link inside, or choose **"Resend verification email"** to get a new one. Once you've clicked the link, you'll be able to log in normally.

### I never got the email at all.

A few common reasons: it landed in spam/junk, the address was mistyped at sign-up, or your mail provider is delaying it. Try "Resend verification email" first. If it still doesn't arrive after checking spam, our support team can help make sure your address is correct.

> **Important distinction:** Email verification is *not* the same as identity verification. Email verification only confirms your email address so you can log in. Identity verification (covered below) is a separate step where our team reviews your documents so you can list a property or send a rental request.

## 4. Logging In & Password Help

### How do I log in?

Go to the **Login** page and enter the email and password you registered with. If your email is verified, you'll be taken to your dashboard. Your session keeps you signed in as you move around, and refreshes quietly in the background so you're not logged out in the middle of something.

### How long do I stay logged in?

Your session stays active while you're using the platform and renews itself in the background, so normal use won't kick you out. For your security, sessions don't last forever — if you've been away for a long time you may be asked to sign in again. Logging out ends your session immediately.

### I forgot my password.

1. On the login page, choose **"Forgot password."**
2. Enter your email address.
3. If there's an account for that email, we'll send a password-reset link. For your security, you'll see the same confirmation message whether or not the email is registered — so no one can use this to discover which emails have accounts.
4. Open the email, click the link, and set a new password (it must meet the same strength requirements as sign-up).

### The password reset link doesn't work.

Reset links are valid for a limited time and can only be used **once.** If yours has expired or has already been used, simply request a new one from the **"Forgot password"** page. Each new request replaces the previous link.

### How do I log out?

Use the **Log out** option in the menu. This fully ends your session on our side, so it's the safest way to sign off — especially on a shared or public device. Just closing the tab isn't the same as logging out.

### How do I change my password when I'm already logged in?

If you know your current password and just want a new one, you can use the **"Forgot password"** flow with your own email to set a fresh password. If you have any trouble, our support team can help.

### I think someone else may have accessed my account.

Change your password right away using "Forgot password," then log out of other sessions by logging out and back in. If you're still worried, contact our support team so they can help secure your account.

## 5. Identity Verification (Getting Verified)

### What is identity verification?

This is a step where you submit documents that prove who you are, and **our team reviews and approves them.** It's separate from confirming your email. Getting verified unlocks the most important actions on the platform, and you only need to do it once (unless something needs correcting).

### Why do I need to be verified?

- **Tenants** must be verified before they can **send a rental request** for a property.
- **Owners** must be verified before they can **list a property.**

Verification helps keep everyone on the platform genuine and trustworthy, so both tenants and owners can deal with confidence.

### How do I get verified? (step by step)

1. Go to your **Verification** section.
2. Upload the required document(s). The form shows which document types are accepted — typically a government-issued ID, and for owners, documents that support property ownership.
3. Submit your documents for review. Your status changes to **under review.**
4. Our team reviews them.
5. When approved, your status becomes **verified** and the actions you were waiting on (listing a property, or sending a rental request) unlock automatically.
6. If something's wrong, your status becomes **needs attention,** usually with a short note explaining what to fix. Correct it and resubmit.

### What documents are accepted?

The form lists the accepted document types at the point of upload. In general it's a form of identity document, plus — for owners — paperwork that supports the property you intend to list. Upload clear, complete, current documents; the accepted types are always shown to you in the moment so you don't have to guess.

### What file types and sizes can I upload?

Documents are uploaded as files (for example a PDF). There's a size limit per file, and the upload screen will tell you if a file is too large or in an unsupported format. Make sure scans are clear and readable — blurry, cropped, or partial documents are the single most common reason a submission gets sent back.

### How long does verification take?

Our team reviews submissions as quickly as they can. I don't have an exact turnaround time to quote, but you can check your current status anytime in your **Verification** section. If it's taking unusually long, our support team can look into it for you.

### My verification was rejected or sent back. What now?

Don't worry — it's usually something simple to fix. Check the note attached to the decision (for example "the ID photo is blurry, please resubmit"), correct that specific issue, and submit your documents again. Resubmitting puts you back in the review queue — there's no penalty for resubmitting, and you can do it as many times as needed.

### How do I check my verification status?

Open your **Verification** section — it shows whether you're not yet submitted, under review, verified, or needs attention. Check back there for the latest, and once you're verified the actions you were waiting on (listing a property, or sending a rental request) unlock automatically.

### Do I lose my verified status?

No — once you're verified you stay verified for normal use. You won't be asked to re-verify just to send another request or list another property.

> **Important distinction:** People often say "I'm waiting for approval." There are a few different approvals on the platform — email verification (to log in), identity verification (to list or to send a request), and document review (of a lease or cancellation agreement). If you're unsure which one you're waiting on, the assistant can help you figure it out by asking what you were trying to do.

## 6. Roles: Tenant, Owner, and Doing Both

### Can I be both a tenant and an owner?

Yes. You start with one role when you sign up, but if you registered as a tenant and later want to list a property, you can **add the owner ability to your existing account** — no need to create a second account or a second email. Look for the **"Become an owner"** option in your account or dashboard.

### What's the difference between a tenant and an owner?

- **Tenants** browse and compare listings, schedule visits, send rental requests, sign leases, pay rent and other charges, and can raise maintenance complaints on their active lease.
- **Owners** list properties, review and respond to rental requests, draw up leases, apply charges (like rent), track payments, and handle maintenance issues on their properties.

### How do I become an owner? (step by step)

1. Sign in to your account.
2. Choose **"Become an owner."** This adds owner abilities to your account immediately.
3. Before you can actually **list** a property, make sure you're **identity-verified** (see Section 5) — verification is required to list.
4. Head to **"My Properties"** to create your first listing.

### If I become an owner, do I lose my tenant abilities?

No. Adding the owner role doesn't remove anything — you can keep renting as a tenant and list properties as an owner from the same account. The platform shows you the right tools for whatever you're doing.

## 7. Browsing & Finding Properties

### How do I browse listings?

Anyone can browse — you don't even need an account to look. Open the **Properties** or listings page to see available homes, newest first. Each listing shows photos, the location, the monthly rent, any upfront amounts, and a short description.

### How do I move through lots of listings?

Listings are shown in pages so they load quickly, with newer ones first. Use the paging controls to go through more results. Open any listing that catches your eye for the full detail view.

### What do the amounts on a listing mean?

- **Monthly rent** — what you'll pay each month.
- **Upfront payment** — an amount due at the start, if the owner requires one.
- **Security deposit** — a refundable amount held against damage or unpaid dues, returned according to the lease terms when you leave.

Exact amounts vary by property and are always shown on the listing and again in any rental request or lease, so you can see them before committing to anything.

### How do I see the full details of a property?

Click a listing to open its detail page, where you'll find all the photos, the full description, the address, the pricing breakdown, and options to **schedule a visit** or **send a rental request** (if you're signed in and verified).

### Why is a property marked as unavailable or occupied?

- **Available** — open for rental requests.
- **Occupied** — someone already has an active lease on it.
- **Unavailable** — the owner has temporarily taken it off the market.

You can only send a rental request for a property that's currently **available.** If a place you like is occupied or unavailable, you can keep an eye on it in case its status changes.

### Can I search for specific things (area, price)?

You can browse the listings and open the ones that fit what you're after. For anything you can't find, or to be notified about particular kinds of listings, our support team can point you in the right direction.

## 8. Comparing Properties

### Can I compare properties side by side?

Yes. As you browse, you can **select properties to compare** and then open the **Compare** view to see them next to each other — rent, deposits, location, and other details lined up so you can weigh them at a glance instead of flipping between tabs.

### How do I compare, step by step?

1. While browsing, mark the listings you're interested in for comparison (for example with a "compare" or "add to compare" control on each listing).
2. Once you've selected at least **two,** open the **Compare** view.
3. Review the properties side by side.
4. Add or remove properties to refine the comparison, then head back to send a request or schedule a visit on your favourite.

### How many properties can I compare at once?

You can compare **up to three properties at a time.** If you try to add a fourth, you'll be prompted to remove one first. You need at least **two** selected for the comparison to be meaningful.

### How do I remove a property from my comparison?

Deselect it from the listing, or remove it directly in the Compare view. You can also **clear your whole selection** to start over. Your selections stay put as you move around the site during your visit, so you won't lose them just by clicking into a listing and back.

### Do my comparison selections stay if I leave?

They persist while you're browsing during your visit. If you fully close the site and come back later, you may need to re-select. It's a quick shortlist tool, not permanent storage.

## 9. Scheduling a Site Visit

### Can I visit a property before renting?

Yes. On a property's detail page you can **request a site visit** — pick a date (and time) you'd like to view the home, and send the request to the owner. It's a good way to see a place in person before you commit to a rental request.

### How does the visit request work, step by step?

1. Open the property and choose to **schedule / request a visit.**
2. Pick the date (and time) you'd like to come.
3. Submit the request. It goes to the owner and starts out as **pending.**
4. The owner **approves** it (confirming the visit) or declines it.
5. Check your **Site Visits** area to see their response and the status of all your visit requests.

### Can I cancel a site visit?

Yes. While a visit is still pending or approved, you can **cancel** it from your **Site Visits** area. It's courteous to cancel if your plans change so the owner isn't left waiting.

### What do the visit statuses mean?

- **Pending** — you've requested it and the owner hasn't responded yet.
- **Approved** — the owner has confirmed the visit.
- **Cancelled** — the visit was called off (by you or the owner).

### Where do I see my scheduled visits?

- **Tenants** see their visit requests in the tenant **Site Visits** section.
- **Owners** see incoming visit requests for their properties in the owner **Site Visits** section, where they can approve, decline, or add remarks.

Both views update as statuses change — check your **Site Visits** area to see the latest on any visit you've requested or received.

### I'm an owner — how do I respond to visit requests?

Open your **Site Visits** section to see incoming requests. You can **approve** a request to confirm the viewing, or decline it, and you can add a remark (for example to suggest a better time). The response shows in the tenant's Site Visits area.

## 10. Sending a Rental Request (Tenants)

### How do I request to rent a property? (step by step)

1. Make sure you're **logged in and identity-verified** — you can't send a request otherwise.
2. Open the property and choose **"Request to Rent"** (sometimes shown as sending a proposal).
3. Enter the terms you're proposing: your desired **start and end dates** and the **amounts** (monthly rent, any upfront payment, and security deposit).
4. Submit it to the owner. It then appears in your **"My Requests"** list, and the owner sees it in their received requests.

### What are the rules for the dates and amounts?

- The **start date** must be today or later — you can't pick a date in the past.
- The **end date** must be after the start date, and the lease must run for **at least one month.**
- The **amounts** are all required. You can propose values to the owner, and some amounts can be zero if that's genuinely the arrangement — but every amount field must be filled in (not left blank).

### Can I negotiate the terms with the owner?

The request you send carries the terms you're proposing. The owner reviews them and can accept or reject the request. When the lease itself is drawn up, the final agreed terms are set there. If you want to adjust terms before an owner accepts, you can withdraw a request and send a new one, or discuss it with the owner.

### Why can't I send a rental request?

The most common reasons are:

- **You're not verified yet.** Identity verification is required before you can send a request. Once our team approves your documents, the option unlocks.
- **The property isn't available** — it may already be occupied or taken off the market.
- **Your dates aren't valid** — the start date must be today or later, and the lease at least a month long.

If you've checked all of these and still can't send a request, our support team can help.

### Can I edit a rental request after creating it?

While a request is still a **draft** (before you've submitted it to the owner), you can edit its terms freely. Once you submit it to the owner for review, it's locked in — if you need different terms after that, you'd typically withdraw it and send a new one, or discuss adjustments with the owner directly.

### What are the stages a request goes through?

- **Draft** — you're still preparing it; only you can see it, and you can edit it.
- **Submitted** — sent to the owner and waiting on their response.
- **Approved** — the owner accepted it; they can now create a lease from it.
- **Rejected** — the owner declined it.
- **Withdrawn** — you cancelled it.
- **Expired** — it sat without action for a long time and was closed automatically.

### How do I track my requests?

Open **"My Requests."** Each request shows its current stage, so you always know whether you're waiting on the owner, cleared to move forward, or need to send a fresh one.

### Can I withdraw a rental request?

Yes, in most cases:

- If it's still a **draft** or **submitted** (waiting on the owner), you can withdraw it anytime.
- If the owner has **approved** it, you can still withdraw it **as long as the lease process hasn't actually started.** Once the owner has begun drawing up the lease, the request can no longer be withdrawn — at that point you'd continue the conversation directly with the owner or reach out to support.

Withdrawing an approved request prevents the owner from creating a lease from it, so you'll be asked to **confirm** before it goes through.

### I tried to withdraw but it wouldn't let me.

That usually means the owner has already started the lease based on your approved request, so it's too late to withdraw. If your plans have changed, talk to the owner, or contact our support team if you need help sorting it out.

### What does it mean if my request "expired"?

If a request sits without being acted on for a long time, the system may mark it as **expired** to keep things tidy. If that happens and you're still interested, you can simply send a fresh request.

### Can I send requests for more than one property?

Yes — you can send requests for several properties while you decide. Just remember that each one, if approved, could turn into a lease, so withdraw the ones you no longer want.

## 11. Listing a Property (Owners)

### How do I list a property? (step by step)

1. Make sure you have the **owner** ability on your account and that you're **identity-verified** — both are required to list.
2. Go to **"My Properties"** and choose to **add a new property.**
3. Fill in the details: title, description, address, city, monthly rent, any upfront payment, and the security deposit.
4. **Upload photos,** and choose the order they appear in.
5. **Attach the property document(s)** required for verification (such as ownership paperwork).
6. Save it. At first it's a **draft** that only you can see.
7. **Submit it for verification.** Our team reviews it, and once approved it goes **live** for tenants to find.

### What details does a listing need?

A clear title, a description, the full address and city, and the pricing (monthly rent, any upfront payment, and security deposit). Good photos make a big difference — you can upload several and set the order they appear in, so lead with your best shot.

### What are the photo requirements?

You can upload multiple images in common formats (like JPEG or PNG). There's a size limit per image, and the upload screen will flag anything too large or in an unsupported format. A clear cover photo plus a few room shots works best and tends to attract more interest.

### Why does my property need a document before I can submit it?

To be verified, a listing needs the appropriate ownership/property document attached (for example proof of ownership). If you try to submit without it, you'll be prompted to **upload the document first.** This is how our team confirms the listing is legitimate, which protects tenants and keeps your listing credible.

### How do the property verification stages work?

- **Draft** — only you can see it; still being prepared, and you can edit it freely.
- **Under review** — you've submitted it and our team is checking it.
- **Verified (live)** — approved and visible to tenants, open for rental requests.
- **Needs attention** — sent back, usually with a note on what to fix. Correct it and resubmit.

### How long does listing verification take?

Our team reviews listings as quickly as they can. I don't have an exact time to quote — you can check the listing's status anytime under **"My Properties,"** and once it's verified it goes live for tenants automatically.

### My listing was sent back. What do I fix?

Check the note attached to the decision — it usually points to the specific issue (for example a missing or unclear document, or details that need correcting). Fix that, then resubmit. There's no limit on resubmitting.

### Can I edit or remove a listing?

Yes. You can update a listing's details and photos, and you can delete it. You can also mark a property as **unavailable** to temporarily take it off the market without deleting it — handy if it's between tenants or you're not ready to accept requests.

### What happens to a listing once a tenant moves in?

When a lease becomes active on a property, the property is marked **occupied** so it's no longer open for new requests. When the lease ends (either it expires or is cancelled), the property automatically becomes **available** again.

### Where do I manage my listings?

Everything is under **"My Properties"** — create, edit, submit for verification, mark availability, and see each property's current status at a glance.

## 12. Reviewing Rental Requests (Owners)

### Where do I see requests from tenants?

Open **"Received Requests."** You'll see every rental request across all your properties, along with the tenant's details and the terms they've proposed, newest first.

### How do I respond to a request?

You can **accept** a request (which lets you move forward and create a lease from it) or **reject** it. The tenant is notified either way. If the proposed terms aren't quite right, you and the tenant can align on terms before you finalize the lease.

### What happens after I accept a request?

Accepting it approves the terms and lets you **create a formal lease** based on that request. From there the lease goes through preparation, review, signing, and a final check before it becomes active (see **Leases** below). Accepting doesn't finalize anything on its own — the lease steps do.

### Can I accept more than one request for the same property?

A property can only have one active lease at a time. It's best to accept and move forward with the request you intend to rent to, and reject the others so tenants aren't left waiting. Once a lease is active, the property is marked occupied.

### Do I have to respond right away?

There's no need to rush, but responding promptly is good practice — tenants may be considering other places, and requests that sit untouched for a long time can expire automatically.

## 13. Leases (Signing & Managing)

### How does a lease get created?

Once an owner has accepted a tenant's request, the **owner draws up the lease** with the agreed terms and attaches the lease agreement document. The lease then moves through a few clear stages before it becomes active — a review of the agreement, the tenant signing, and a final review — so both sides are protected and everything is on record.

### What are the stages a lease goes through? (in plain terms)

1. **Being prepared** — the owner is setting up the lease and its agreement document. The owner can still make edits at this stage.
2. **Under review** — the owner has submitted the lease agreement, and it's being checked before it goes to the tenant.
3. **Awaiting your signature** — it's approved and ready for the **tenant** to sign.
4. **Signed, under final review** — the tenant has uploaded the signed agreement, and it's getting a final check.
5. **Active** — everything's approved; the lease is live, the property is marked occupied, and rent and charges can be handled.

You'll be notified as it moves from one stage to the next, and you can always check the current stage on the lease itself.

### What if the agreement doesn't pass review?

If the lease agreement or the signed copy doesn't pass a review, the lease is sent back rather than continuing. The owner (and tenant, where relevant) can address what's needed and move it forward again. If you're unsure what to change, our support team can help.

### How do I sign my lease (tenant)? (step by step)

1. When your lease reaches the **awaiting signature** stage, open it from **"My Leases."**
2. Review the lease agreement document carefully.
3. Sign it, and **upload the signed copy** back to the lease.
4. It then goes through a **final review** before becoming active.
5. You can also attach your own supporting documents to the lease if needed.

You'll be notified when the lease becomes active.

### Where do I find my leases?

Open **"My Leases."** Owners see leases for their properties; tenants see the leases they're party to. Each one shows its current stage and the key terms (dates and amounts).

### Can I download the lease documents?

Yes — the lease agreement and any attached documents are available from the lease's detail view for both the owner and the tenant, so you each keep a copy of what was agreed.

### Can the terms change after the lease is active?

The core lease terms are set when the lease is finalized. Ongoing amounts like rent are handled through **charges** on the active lease (see Section 14). To end a lease early or change the arrangement significantly, you'd use a **cancellation** (Section 17). For anything unusual, contact our support team.

### Does a lease end automatically when the term is over?

## 14. Charges, Deposits & Paying Rent

### What are "charges"?

Charges are amounts the **owner** adds to an **active** lease for the tenant to pay. Think of them as line items on your rental account — each one has a **type,** an **amount,** an optional **description,** and a **due date.** As you pay, each charge tracks how much has been paid and how much is still owed. Only the owner of the property can create charges, and only on a lease that's already active.

### What kinds of charges are there? (charge types explained)

- **Monthly rent** — your regular rent for each period, added by the owner as it comes due.
- **Security deposit** — a refundable amount held against damage or unpaid dues; it's returned according to your lease terms when the lease ends (see below).
- **Upfront payment** — a one-time amount due at the start of the lease, if your arrangement includes one.
- **Maintenance** — a charge for repairs or upkeep that, under your lease, is your responsibility (for example a specific repair cost the owner passes on).
- **Penalty** — a charge the owner adds for something like a late or missed payment, or another breach of the lease terms (see "How do penalties work?" below).
- **Other** — anything that doesn't fit the categories above; the description explains what it's for.

Each charge shows its type and description, so you always know what you're being asked to pay and why.

### How do penalties work?

A **penalty** is simply another type of charge that the **owner** chooses to add — for example if rent was paid late, a payment was missed, or some other term of the lease wasn't met. A few things to know:

- **Penalties are not applied automatically by the platform.** The owner decides whether to add one, for how much, and gives it a description and a due date, just like any other charge.
- A penalty appears in your charges list like any other line item, and you pay it the same way (online by card or by recording a manual payment).
- The **reason** should be in the charge's description. If a penalty appears that you don't understand or think is a mistake, the best first step is to raise it with the owner — you can also use a **complaint** under the "Billing/Payment" category to discuss it in a shared thread.
- The platform doesn't set penalty amounts or decide when they're fair — those come from your lease agreement and the owner. If you and the owner can't agree, our support team can help point you in the right direction, though disputes about money owed are ultimately between the two parties.

### What happens if I miss a due date? (overdue charges)

Every charge has a **due date.** If a charge isn't fully paid by its due date, it's marked **overdue** so both you and the owner can see it clearly. Being overdue doesn't automatically add extra fees on its own — but an owner may choose to add a separate **penalty** charge for a late or missed payment, depending on your lease terms. To avoid that, pay charges before their due date, or reach out to the owner if you need to arrange something. You can filter your charges to see just the outstanding or overdue ones.

### How does the security deposit work?

The **security deposit** is a refundable amount tied to your lease. It's held for the duration of the lease and returned to you when the lease ends, minus anything owed under your lease terms (for example unpaid charges or agreed deductions). When a lease is **cancelled,** the agreed **deposit refund amount** and the reasons for any deductions are written into the cancellation agreement, so you have a clear record of what's being returned and why. Deposit disputes (over the amount refunded, damage, or deductions) are handled by our support team, since they can review your lease and cancellation details securely.

### What currency are charges in?

Amounts are handled in the platform's standard currency (Indian Rupees by default). The amount shown on each charge is what's owed in that currency.

### How do I pay rent or another charge (tenant)? (step by step)

1. Open your lease to see its charges.
2. Pick the charge(s) you want to pay and **how much to put toward each** — a single payment can cover several charges at once, in full or in part.
3. Choose how you're paying: **online by card,** or by **recording a manual payment** (for example a bank transfer you made outside the platform).
4. Confirm. The charge updates to show how much has been paid and how much is still due.

### Can one payment cover several charges?

Yes. You can split a single payment across multiple charges and decide how much goes toward each — helpful when you want to clear, say, rent and a maintenance charge together.

### Can I pay part of a charge?

Yes. You can pay a charge partially; it will show as **partially paid** with the remaining balance still due, and you can pay the rest later.

### What's the difference between paying online and recording a manual payment?

- **Online by card** — you pay right there on the platform through a secure, trusted payment provider. Any applicable fee is shown clearly before you confirm.
- **Manual payment** — you record a payment you made by another method (like a direct bank transfer), including a reference for it, so the lease's records stay accurate for both you and the owner.

### Is paying online safe?

Yes. Online card payments are handled by a **secure, trusted payment provider.** Your full card details aren't stored on the platform, and you'll always see the total — including any fee — before you confirm.

### What are the charge statuses?

- **Pending** — added but not yet paid.
- **Partially paid** — some of the amount has been paid; a balance remains.
- **Paid** — fully settled.
- **Overdue** — its due date has passed and it isn't fully paid yet.
- **Cancelled** — the owner cancelled the charge, so it's no longer owed.

A charge moves through these on its own as things happen — for example it becomes **partially paid** or **paid** as you pay, and **overdue** once the due date passes without full payment.

### How do I see what I owe or what I've paid?

Open the lease — each charge shows the amount, how much has been paid, and the remaining balance. There's also a **payment history** for the lease that both the tenant and owner can see, so records stay transparent on both sides.

### How do I apply a charge to a tenant (owner)? (step by step)

1. Open the **active** lease.
2. Add a charge: choose the **type** (rent, security deposit, upfront payment, maintenance, penalty, or other), enter the **amount** and a clear **description,** and set a **due date** (which must be today or later).
3. Save it. The tenant will then see the charge and can pay against it, and you'll both see its status update as payments come in.

### How do I add a penalty or a maintenance charge (owner)?

The same way as any other charge — choose the **penalty** or **maintenance** type when you add it. Always put the **reason** in the description (for example "late rent for August" or "cost of repairing the water heater") so the tenant understands exactly what it's for and why. Clear descriptions prevent confusion and disputes. Penalties are entirely at the owner's discretion within the lease terms — the platform doesn't add them automatically.

### Can an owner cancel a charge?

Yes. If a charge was added by mistake or is no longer owed, the owner can **cancel** it, and it will show as cancelled and no longer count toward what the tenant owes. Amounts a tenant has already paid are part of the payment history.

### Can an owner charge before the lease is active?

Charges apply to leases that are **active.** Until a lease is fully finalized and active, there's nothing to charge against. Once it's active, the owner can add rent and other charges as needed.

### Is there a fee for paying online?

A small fee may apply to online card payments. The exact amount is always shown clearly before you confirm, so you'll see the full total up front. I don't have the specific percentage to quote here — if you'd like it confirmed in advance, our support team can help.

### My online payment failed or didn't go through.

Payments that don't complete are marked as failed and the charge stays unpaid, so you can safely try again. If money left your account but the charge still shows unpaid, don't pay twice — contact our support team with the details so they can check it securely.

## 17. Ending a Lease Early (Cancellation)

### How do I end a lease before it's over?

An **active** lease can be ended early through a **cancellation.** It can start from either side:

- **Tenant-initiated:** the tenant submits a **cancellation request,** and the owner reviews it.
- **Owner-initiated:** the owner starts the cancellation directly.

Either way, the cancellation then goes through the same kind of **agreement-review-and-signing** process as a lease, so both sides agree to the terms (including any security deposit refund) before it's final.

### How does a tenant request cancellation? (step by step)

1. Open your **active** lease and choose to **request cancellation.**
2. Give a **reason,** and the **date you'd like it to take effect** (and, optionally, your planned move-out date).
3. Submit it. The owner will review and either **accept** or **decline** it.
4. If the owner accepts, a cancellation agreement is prepared, you'll **sign it,** and after a **final review** the lease is formally ended.
5. You'll be notified at each step.

### What are the rules for a cancellation request?

- The lease must be **active.**
- You provide a reason and the date you'd like it to take effect (which can't be in the past).
- Your move-out date, if you give one, should be on or after the effective date.

### How does an owner cancel? (step by step)

1. From the active lease, start a cancellation (or accept a tenant's cancellation request, which sets one up for you).
2. Fill in the details — reason, effective date, optional move-out date, and any **security deposit refund amount** and notes.
3. Attach the cancellation agreement and submit it for review.
4. The tenant signs it, and after a final review the lease is terminated.

### What happens when a cancellation is finalized?

Once everything's signed and approved, the lease is marked as **terminated** and the property becomes **available** again. Any agreed **security deposit refund** and related terms are recorded as part of the cancellation agreement, so both sides have a clear record of what was settled.

### Can only one cancellation happen at a time?

Yes — a lease can only have one active cancellation (or cancellation request) in progress at a time. Finish or resolve the current one before starting another.

### Can I withdraw a cancellation request?

There isn't a self-service option to withdraw a cancellation request once it's submitted. If you've changed your mind, the best step is to talk to the other party or reach out to our support team, who can help.

## 18. Complaints & Maintenance Issues

### How do I report a problem with my rental (tenant)?

If you have an **active** lease, you can raise a **complaint** about an issue with the property. Categories include maintenance, plumbing, electrical, appliances, noise/neighbours, security/safety, billing/payment, and other.

**Step by step:**

1. Open the complaints section and start a **new complaint** on your active lease.
2. Choose a **category** and a **priority** (low, medium, high, or urgent).
3. Add a **subject** and a **description** of the problem.
4. Optionally **attach a photo or document** (like a picture of a leak) to explain it.
5. Submit it — the owner (and, where appropriate, our team) will see it and can respond.

### How do I set the priority?

Pick the level that matches how serious it is: **urgent** for something dangerous or making the home unusable (like a major leak or a safety issue), **high** for pressing problems, and **medium/low** for less time-sensitive things. Honest priorities help owners respond appropriately.

### Can I attach a photo to a complaint?

Yes. You can attach a photo or document to help explain the issue. There's a size limit, and the upload screen will tell you if a file is too large or in an unsupported format.

### How do complaints get resolved?

A complaint moves through clear stages:

- **Open** — just raised.
- **In progress** — being worked on.
- **Resolved** — the fix is done.
- **Closed** — the person who raised it confirms it's finished (this is final).
- **Cancelled** — it's no longer needed (this is final).

You and the owner can add comments back and forth in a **shared thread** to work it out. Once resolved, the person who raised it can **close** it, or it can be **reopened** if the problem comes back.

### Can I keep discussing a complaint after it's opened?

Yes — everyone involved can add comments to the complaint's thread while it's active, so it works like an ongoing conversation. Once a complaint is **closed or cancelled,** the thread is finished and no more comments can be added.

### Can I reopen a complaint if the problem comes back?

Yes — if a complaint was resolved but the issue returns, it can be moved back to **in progress** so you can pick the conversation back up. Complaints that have been fully **closed or cancelled** are final, so for a recurring issue after that, raise a new complaint.

### Where do I see complaints?

- **Tenants** see the complaints they've raised under **"My Complaints."**
- **Owners** see complaints on their properties under their received-complaints view.

Open any complaint to read the full conversation thread and add a comment.

### I'm an owner — how do I handle a complaint?

Open the complaint from your received complaints, read the details and any attachment, and use the thread to communicate with the tenant. As you work on it, move it to **in progress,** then **resolved** once it's fixed. You can add a note with each update so the tenant stays informed.

## 19. Notifications & How You're Kept Informed

### How will I know when something happens?

### What exactly do I get notified about (in-app)?

You'll receive a notification for the important moments on both sides of a deal. Here's the full picture:

**Rental requests**
- When a tenant **sends you a rental request** (the owner is notified).
- When an owner **accepts** your rental request (the tenant is notified).
- When an owner **rejects** your rental request (the tenant is notified).

**Leases**
- When your lease agreement has been **reviewed and is ready for you to sign** (the tenant is notified).
- When a lease agreement is **sent back for changes** during review (the owner is notified).
- When your **signed lease is approved and the lease becomes active** (you're notified).
- When a signed lease is **sent back** and needs attention (you're notified).
- When a lease **expires** at the end of its term — both the owner and tenant are notified, and the property is freed up automatically.

**Lease cancellations**
- When a tenant **submits a cancellation request** (the owner is notified).
- When an owner **accepts** or **rejects** a cancellation request (the tenant is notified).
- When a cancellation agreement has been **reviewed and is ready to sign** (the tenant is notified).
- When a cancellation agreement or signed copy is **sent back** for changes (the owner is notified).
- When a cancellation is **finalized** and the lease is formally ended (both parties are notified).

Each notification links you straight to the relevant place — for example a rental request notification takes the owner to their received requests, and a "ready to sign" notification takes the tenant to the lease.

### Do I get notified about site visits and complaints?

- **Site visits:** the response to a visit request (approved or declined) is shown in your **Site Visits** area, and its status updates there — so check that section to see where a visit stands.
- **Complaints:** activity on a complaint happens in the complaint's **shared comment thread.** Open the complaint from **"My Complaints"** (tenants) or your received complaints (owners) to read new comments and status changes.

For these two areas, the most reliable way to stay on top of things is to open the relevant section, rather than waiting on a push alert.

### What comes by email?

Emails are reserved for account and security steps:

- Your **email verification link** when you register (and again if you use "Resend verification email").
- Your **password reset link** when you use "Forgot password."

Day-to-day activity (requests, leases, payments, cancellations) is delivered through **in-app notifications** rather than email, so it's worth checking your notifications and the relevant sections when you log in.

### Where do I find my notifications, and how do I clear them?

Open the **Notifications** area (usually a bell icon or menu item). It lists your recent updates newest-first, and shows how many are unread. Opening or dismissing a notification marks it as **read** so you can keep your list tidy and focus on what's new.

### Will I be told when a charge is added or a payment is made?

Charges and payments are always visible on the lease itself — open the lease to see new charges, their due dates, what's been paid, and the balance due. Both the owner and tenant see the same up-to-date charge list and payment history, so you can check your rental account at any time.
